Hi there! I'm Tiffany.

I am a first-year PhD student in the UC Berkeley Statistics Department. Previously, I received an ScB in Applied Mathematics and an ScM in Computer Science from Brown University, where I was fortunate to work with Elizabeth Chen, Chip Lawrence, and Stephen Bach. I have also spent summers at Facebook and the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory.

Feel free to connect with me via email at tiffany_ding[at]berkeley[dot]edu or on LinkedIn.

News

  • January 2022: Our paper on weakly supervised learning using noisy partial labelers was accepted to AISTATS 2022.
  • December 2021: I presented a poster at the AGU 2021 Fall Meeting on my research (with Taehee Lee, Lorraine Lisiecki, and Charles Lawrence) about using Gaussian processes and state space models to infer historical sea levels.
  • May 2021: I was awarded the Jerome L. Stein Memorial Award for Undergraduate Excellence by Brown University's Dept. of Applied Math.
  • April 2021: I presented my honors thesis on applying Gaussian processes and state space models to infer historical sea levels using oxygen isotope records.
  • October 2020: I contributed a portrait of Dr. Sijue Wu (of the University of Michigan) to the Association of Women in Mathematics' EvenQuads playing cards project, which celebrates female mathematicians.
  • September 2020: Soryan Kumar and I won 2nd place in the Citadel East Coast Regional Datathon for our analysis of the optimal target audience for maximizing movie profitability.
